🏛 Vidhana Soudha

📜 Overview

Vidhana Soudha is the seat of the Karnataka State Legislature. Built in 1956 under the vision of Kengal Hanumanthaiah, this majestic granite building is a blend of Neo-Dravidian, Indo-Saracenic, and Dravidian styles. Often dubbed the "Taj Mahal of South India" (architecturally), it stands as a symbol of democratic governance in India.

🌟 Highlights

  • Sprawls across 60 acres and stands 46 meters tall
  • Contains over 300 rooms for various legislative operations
  • Features a grand central dome crowned with the Indian National Emblem
  • Beautifully illuminated on Sundays and public holidays
  • India’s largest legislative building

🧭 Visitor Information

🚫 Public Access: General public is not allowed inside without prior permission
📸 Exterior Viewing: Open 24/7 from the outside
🕒 Office Hours: Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM
💡 Lighting: Evenings on Sundays & public holidays
💰 Entry Fee: Free for exterior viewing; interior access requires special passes
