🐂 Bull Temple (Dodda Basavana Gudi)

📜 Overview

The Bull Temple, or Dodda Basavana Gudi, is a 16th-century temple located in Basavanagudi, Bengaluru. Built by Kempe Gowda I, the founder of Bangalore, it is dedicated to Nandi, the sacred bull and vehicle of Lord Shiva. The temple exemplifies traditional Dravidian architecture and plays a vital role in the city's spiritual and cultural identity.

✨ Highlights

  • Features a monolithic Nandi statue carved from a single granite rock (4.6m high, 6.1m long).
  • Decorated year-round with garlands and flowers by devotees.
  • Hosts the iconic Kadalekai Parishe (Groundnut Festival) every November.
  • Located beside the tranquil Bugle Rock Park.

🧭 Visitor Information

🕒 Timings: Daily, 6:00 AM – 8:00 PM
🌅 Best Time: Early morning or evening, especially during special poojas

🎟 Entry Fee: Free for all visitors
💰 Donations: Optional at designated counters
📷 Photography: Allowed outside the sanctum (with discretion)
