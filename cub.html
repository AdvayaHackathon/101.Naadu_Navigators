Cubbon Park

Bangalore’s Green Heart

🌳 Overview

Cubbon Park is a historic and lush green oasis in the heart of Bangalore, Karnataka. Spanning over 300 acres, it serves as a peaceful retreat and a biodiversity hotspot amid the urban hustle. Established in 1870, the park is named after Sir Mark Cubbon, the British commissioner of Mysore.

🌟 Highlights
  • 🏛 Attara Kacheri – High Court of Karnataka in Gothic-style architecture
  • 📚 Seshadri Iyer Memorial Hall – State Central Library
  • 🐠 Bangalore Aquarium – India’s second-oldest aquarium
  • 🗿 Iconic statues – Queen Victoria, King Edward VII, Sir Mark Cubbon
  • 🌴 Rich flora – 6,000+ trees of 96 indigenous & exotic species
  • 🚶 Walking & jogging tracks
  • 🎠 Children’s play areas
  • 🏛 Nearby Museums – Visvesvaraya Industrial and Technological Museum
