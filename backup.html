Lalbagh Botanical Garden - Bengaluru

Overview: Lalbagh is one of the most iconic tourist attractions in Bengaluru. It’s a vast botanical garden spread across 240 acres, originally laid out in the 18th century by Hyder Ali and expanded by his son Tipu Sultan. Lalbagh is home to over 1,800 species of plants, trees, and herbs, including some rare and centuries-old trees.

🌟 Highlights:

  • Glass House: Inspired by London’s Crystal Palace, hosts the popular Independence Day & Republic Day flower shows.
  • Lalbagh Rock: One of Earth’s oldest rock formations (3,000+ million years).
  • Lake View: Peaceful lake with walking paths and birdwatching.
  • Nurseries & Greenhouses: Plant sales, seed exchanges, and guided walks.
