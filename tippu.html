🏰 Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace

🕌 Overview

Tipu Sultan’s Summer Palace is a magnificent Indo-Islamic structure nestled in the heart of Bangalore. Built in the late 18th century, this wooden marvel served as the seasonal retreat of Tipu Sultan, the renowned ruler of Mysore. Constructed mostly from teakwood, the palace features ornate pillars, arched balconies, and stunning hand-carved motifs.

✨ Highlights

  • Wooden Architecture: Crafted from teakwood with ornamental arches and pillars.
  • Historic Relevance: Glimpses into Tipu Sultan’s rule and anti-colonial legacy.
  • Frescoes & Murals: Preserved floral and artistic patterns from the 18th century.
  • Museum: Exhibits of Tipu’s personal artifacts, weapons, and history.
  • Serene Gardens: Lush greenery creates a calm, peaceful atmosphere.
